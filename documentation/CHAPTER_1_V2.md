# CHAPTER ONE: INTRODUCTION

The issue of missing persons is a critical social and humanitarian challenge faced by countries across the world. Every year, thousands of individuals go missing due to various reasons such as accidents, natural disasters, human trafficking, mental health issues, migration, and criminal activities. Locating missing persons using traditional methods is often time-consuming, labor-intensive, and highly dependent on manual processes such as physical searches, paper records, and public announcements. These conventional approaches frequently suffer from delays, lack of coordination among authorities, and limited utilization of modern technological tools, reducing the chances of successful and timely recovery.

With the rapid advancement of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) technologies, new opportunities have emerged to enhance search, identification, and data analysis processes. AI techniques such as face recognition, image processing, pattern matching, and data analytics can significantly improve the efficiency and accuracy of identifying missing persons. These technologies are capable of analyzing large volumes of images and personal data in a short time, making them highly suitable for addressing the limitations of traditional missing person investigation systems.

The AI-Integrated Missing Person Finder project aims to design and develop an intelligent system that assists law enforcement agencies, humanitarian organizations, and the general public in identifying and locating missing individuals more effectively. The proposed system integrates AI-based face recognition with a centralized database that stores missing person records, including photographs and personal details. When a new image is uploaded, such as from CCTV footage, public cameras, or user submissions, the system automatically compares it with stored records and provides potential matches based on similarity scores.

This system not only reduces human effort but also accelerates the identification process by providing real-time or near real-time results. In addition, the platform enables easy reporting of missing persons and sightings through a user-friendly interface, thereby encouraging public participation and collaboration with authorities. By combining AI technology with structured data management and secure access control, the system ensures accuracy, reliability, and privacy of sensitive information.

In conclusion, the AI-Integrated Missing Person Finder represents a modern, technology-driven solution to a serious societal problem. By leveraging artificial intelligence, the proposed system enhances the effectiveness of missing person investigations, increases recovery success rates, and contributes positively to public safety and social welfare. This project demonstrates how AI can be applied to solve real-world problems and highlights its potential impact in humanitarian and security-related domains.

## 1.1 Background of the Project

The problem of missing persons has long been a major concern for governments, law enforcement agencies, and humanitarian organizations worldwide. Traditional approaches to handling missing person cases primarily rely on manual reporting, physical searches, public notices, and human-led investigations. While these methods have been used for decades, they often suffer from limitations such as slow response times, fragmented data storage, lack of real-time information sharing, and heavy dependence on human effort and judgment.

In many regions, missing person records are maintained in isolated databases or paper-based systems, making it difficult to efficiently cross-reference data across different jurisdictions or organizations. Additionally, identification processes often require manual comparison of photographs or physical descriptions, which is both time-consuming and prone to human error. As the volume of reported cases increases, these limitations significantly reduce the effectiveness of traditional missing person management systems.

Recent advancements in Artificial Intelligence (AI), particularly in the areas of computer vision, face recognition, and machine learning, have introduced new possibilities for improving identification and search processes. AI-based systems are capable of automatically analyzing facial features, detecting patterns, and matching images against large datasets with high accuracy. Such technologies are already being applied successfully in domains like security surveillance, identity verification, and criminal investigations.

The widespread availability of digital cameras, smartphones, CCTV systems, and social media platforms has resulted in massive amounts of visual data that can be leveraged for missing person identification. However, without intelligent systems to process and analyze this data, its potential remains largely untapped. This gap between data availability and effective utilization highlights the need for an integrated, AI-driven solution.

The AI-Integrated Missing Person Finder project emerges from this background as a response to the growing demand for faster, more accurate and technology-driven methods of locating missing individuals. By combining AI-powered face recognition with a centralized digital database and user-friendly interfaces, the project seeks to modernize the missing person investigation process. The system is designed to assist authorities in decision-making, enable public participation, and improve coordination between stakeholders while maintaining data security and privacy.

To sum up, this project is grounded in the evolution of traditional investigation methods toward intelligent, automated systems. It reflects the increasing role of artificial intelligence in addressing complex societal problems and demonstrates how modern technology can be applied to enhance public safety and humanitarian efforts.

## 1.2 Statement of the problem and Justification

### 1.2.1 Statement of the Problem
The increasing number of missing person cases presents a serious social, security, and humanitarian challenge. Despite continuous efforts by law enforcement agencies and community organizations, locating missing individuals remains difficult and often unsuccessful, particularly when cases are not resolved within the first critical hours. Existing missing person management systems rely heavily on manual processes such as written reports, physical searches, and human-led photo comparisons, which are slow, inefficient, and susceptible to human error.

One of the major problems is the lack of an intelligent, centralized system capable of automatically analyzing and matching missing person data across large datasets. In many cases, missing person records are stored in fragmented or isolated databases, making it difficult to perform quick cross-referencing between reports, sightings, and visual data sources such as CCTV footage or public images. As a result, valuable time is lost, reducing the likelihood of successful identification and recovery.

Furthermore, the growing availability of digital images and surveillance data is not effectively utilized due to the absence of automated analytical tools. Manual comparison of facial features across thousands of images is practically impossible within a reasonable time frame. This limitation significantly affects investigation efficiency, delays decision-making, and increases operational costs for authorities.

Additionally, public participation in missing person investigations is often unstructured and poorly integrated with official systems. Reports from citizens may not be systematically verified or linked to existing cases, leading to missed opportunities for identification. The absence of a secure and intelligent platform that integrates AI-based analysis with public and institutional data further aggravates the problem.

This project is further justified by the increasing availability of digital data from smartphones, and, which can be effectively leveraged through AI-powered analysis. Moreover, the proposed system promotes social responsibility and public safety by enabling structured public participation while maintaining data privacy and security. It serves as a practical demonstration of how AI can be applied to solve real-world humanitarian problems and aligns with current technological trends in smart policing and digital governance.

Therefore, the AI-Integrated Missing Person Finder is a necessary and relevant solution that addresses the limitations of traditional methods, enhances investigation efficiency, and contributes positively to societal well-being. The project is both technically significant and socially impactful, making it a justified and valuable final-year project.

## 1.3 Objective of the project

### 1.3.1 General objective
The general objective of this project is to design and develop an AI-integrated missing person identification system that utilizes artificial intelligence techniques, particularly face recognition, to efficiently identify, track, and assist in locating missing persons by analyzing and matching images with a centralized database, thereby improving the accuracy, speed, and effectiveness of missing person investigations.

### 1.3.2 Specific objective
1.  Design and implement a centralized database for storing missing person records, including personal details and facial images, in a structured and secure manner.
2.  Develop an AI-based face recognition module capable of extracting facial features from images and matching them against stored records to identify potential matches.
3.  Enable image and sighting uploads from authorized users and the public to support real-time or near real-time identification of missing persons.
4.  Automate the comparison process between newly uploaded images and existing missing person data to reduce manual effort and human error.
5.  Provide similarity scoring and match results to assist authorities in decision-making during investigations.
6.  Design a user-friendly interface that allows easy reporting of missing persons and viewing of match results.
7.  Implement access control and data security mechanisms to ensure privacy and protect sensitive personal information.
8.  Improve coordination and communication between law enforcement agencies, and the public through a unified digital platform.
9.  Evaluate the performance of the system in terms of accuracy, efficiency, and reliability of the AI-based identification process.
10. Demonstrate the practical application of artificial intelligence in solving real-world humanitarian and public safety problems.

## 1.4 Scope of the project

The scope of the AI-Integrated Missing Person Finder project defines the boundaries, functionalities, and limitations of the system. The project focuses on the design and development of an intelligent software solution that leverages artificial intelligence techniques to assist in the identification and tracking of missing persons.

The system covers the creation of a centralized digital database that stores missing person information such as name, age, gender, last known location, physical description, and facial images. It includes the implementation of an AI-based face recognition module that extracts facial features from uploaded images and compares them with stored records to generate potential matches based on similarity scores.

The project scope also includes the development of a web-based user interface that allows authorized users, such as administrators or law enforcement personnel, to manage missing person records. In addition, the system enables the public or registered users to upload images or report sightings, which are then analyzed by the AI module to assist in identification.

Security and privacy considerations are included within the scope through the implementation of user authentication, role-based access control, and secure data storage to protect sensitive personal information. The system is designed to support efficient data retrieval, automated matching, and result visualization to enhance investigation processes.

However, the project scope is limited to assisting identification and providing potential matches rather than guaranteeing the physical recovery of missing persons. The system does not include real-time integration with national surveillance networks, biometric databases. The accuracy of the system depends on the quality and availability of input images and data.

In summary, the project scope encompasses the development of an AI-driven missing person identification system focused on image-based matching, data management, and user interaction, while excluding large-scale deployment, and nationwide surveillance integration.

## 1.5 Limitation of the project

Despite the advantages and potential impact of the AI-Integrated Missing Person Finder, the project has certain limitations that should be acknowledged.

One of the primary limitations is the dependence on image quality and availability. The accuracy of the face recognition system is highly influenced by the clarity, resolution, lighting conditions, and angle of the input images. Poor-quality images, partially covered faces, or outdated photographs may result in inaccurate or missed matches.

The system’s performance is also limited by the size and diversity of the training dataset used for the AI model. A limited dataset may reduce the accuracy of facial recognition, especially for variations in age, facial expressions, hairstyles, or environmental conditions. As a result, the system may not perform equally well for all individuals.

Another limitation is that the system provides potential matches rather than guaranteed identification. Final verification still requires human judgment and manual confirmation by authorities. The system is intended to assist investigations, not replace human decision-making.

The project does not support real-time integration with national surveillance systems, law enforcement databases, or CCTV networks due to technical, legal, and resource constraints. Therefore, image uploads and data analysis relies on manual input rather than continuous automated monitoring.

Privacy and ethical concerns also pose limitations. Strict data protection measures may restrict access to certain information, limiting the extent of data sharing between organizations. Additionally, legal regulations regarding biometric data may affect large-scale deployment.

Finally, the scope of the project is limited to a prototype or academic implementation. Scalability, high-availability infrastructure, and nationwide deployment are beyond the current project scope and require additional resources, funding, and institutional support.

## 1.6 System Development Methodology

System development methodology refers to the structured approach used to plan, design, develop, test, and deploy the proposed system. For the AI-Integrated Missing Person Finder, a systematic methodology was adopted to ensure efficient development, proper integration of artificial intelligence components, and delivery of a reliable and user-friendly system.

### 1.6.1 System Development Approach
The Agile development approach was adopted for this project. Agile is an iterative and incremental development methodology that emphasizes flexibility, continuous improvement, and frequent evaluation of system components. This approach is particularly suitable for AI-based systems, where model performance and system requirements may evolve during development.

Under the Agile approach, the system was developed in multiple iterations, each focusing on a specific module such as database design, user interface development, AI face recognition model implementation, and system integration. After each iteration, testing and evaluation were conducted to identify issues and improve system performance.

The Agile approach allows:
•	Continuous feedback and refinement of system features
•	Early identification of technical challenges
•	Incremental integration of AI components
•	Flexibility to improve model accuracy and system functionality

This approach ensures that the system meets functional requirements while maintaining adaptability to changes during development.

### 1.6.2 System Development Tools
Various hardware and software tools were used during the development of the AI-Integrated Missing Person Finder to ensure efficiency, accuracy, and system reliability.

**A) Programming Languages**
*   **JavaScript (Node.js):** Used for the backend server logic and API handling.
*   **JavaScript (ES6+):** Used for frontend interactivity and DOM manipulation.
*   **Python:** Used for the AI microservice (`ai-service`), specifically for face recognition tasks.
*   **HTML5 & CSS3:** Used for the structure and styling of the user interface.

**B) AI and Machine Learning Tools**
*   **DeepFace:** A lightweight face recognition and facial attribute analysis framework for Python.
*   **TensorFlow / Keras (`tf-keras`):** The underlying deep learning engine used by DeepFace.
*   **OpenCV (`opencv-python`):** Used for image processing and computer vision tasks.
*   **NumPy:** Used for efficient numerical matrix operations on image data.

**C) Database Management System**
*   **MongoDB:** A NoSQL database used to store flexible data models for Users, Missing Persons, and Tips (connected via `mongoose`).

**D) Development Frameworks & Libraries**
*   **Express.js:** A web application framework for Node.js used to build the REST API.
*   **Flask:** A micro-web framework for Python used to expose the AI functionality as a web service.
*   **Mongoose:** An Object Data Modeling (ODM) library for MongoDB types casting and validation.
*   **Font Awesome:** Used for UI icons (found in `index.html`).

**E) Development Environment and Tools**
*   **Visual Studio Code:** The Integrated Development Environment (IDE).
*   **Node.js & npm:** Runtime environment and package manager for the backend.
*   **Python & pip:** Runtime environment and package manager for the AI service.
*   **Render:** Cloud platform configured for hosting the backend/AI services (`render.yaml`).
*   **Netlify:** Platform configured for hosting the frontend static files (`netlify.toml`).
*   **Git:** Version control system (implied by project structure).

**F) Hardware Requirements**
*   **Processor (CPU):** Multi-core processor (Intel Core i5/i7 or AMD Ryzen 5/7) recommended to handle image processing and ML inference.
*   **Memory (RAM):** Minimum 8GB (16GB recommended), as running the DeepFace model and Node server simultaneously can be memory-intensive.
*   **Storage:** SSD (Solid State Drive) recommended for faster read/write speeds, especially for database operations and image storage.
*   **Internet Connection:** broadband connection required for cloud database connectivity and API requests.
*   **Camera:** Webcam or connected camera device for capturing images of missing persons or verification.
